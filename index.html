Dr. Carl Gustav Jung

Tribute to the great Psychiatrist, Psychologist, and founder of Analytical Psychology.

The Search Within

This page is a tribute to the great psychological thinker Dr. Carl Gustav Jung. Born July 26th 1875, and died on June 6th 1961 at age 85. Jung was an important and influential figure in the 20th century, and helped the western world grapple with the psychological analysis of the individual.Many of his ideas had genesis in the thought of his mentor, but Jung would find himself at odds with his teacher, and so the setting for Jung to delve into his own exploration of psychology would be set from this. A student of the famous Sigmund Freud, Jung would take many of Freud's ideas and investigate them arguably much farther than his mentor. Jung argued that the human being is not the master of one’s own house. That is to say, there are forces that compel one unconsciously.Unlike Freud however, Jung was skeptical that human motivations were purely a consequence of sexual drive. Jung suggests it is only through a process called individuation, that one can attain the highest version of themselves. It is through the integration of one’s own unconscious psychic energies that one reaches what Jung called the God Image. Jung drew upon his eclectic knowledge of Philosophy, Psychology, Theology, Myths, Astrology, Alchemy, and many other fields to come to this conclusion.
