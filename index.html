Dr. Carl Gustav Jung

Tribute to the great Psychiatrist, Psychologist, and founder of Analytical Psychology.

The Search Within

This page is a tribute to the great psychological thinker Dr. Carl Gustav Jung. Born July 26th 1875, and died on June 6th 1961 at age 85. Jung was an important and influential figure in the 20th century, and helped the western world grapple with the psychological analysis of the individual.Many of his ideas had genesis in the thought of his mentor, but Jung would find himself at odds with his teacher, and so the setting for Jung to delve into his own exploration of psychology would be set from this. A student of the famous Sigmund Freud, Jung would take many of Freud's ideas and investigate them arguably much farther than his mentor. Jung argued that the human being is not the master of one’s own house. That is to say, there are forces that compel one unconsciously.Unlike Freud however, Jung was skeptical that human motivations were purely a consequence of sexual drive. Jung suggests it is only through a process called individuation, that one can attain the highest version of themselves. It is through the integration of one’s own unconscious psychic energies that one reaches what Jung called the God Image. Jung drew upon his eclectic knowledge of Philosophy, Psychology, Theology, Myths, Astrology, Alchemy, and many other fields to come to this conclusion. This would be the groundwork for what is commonly referred to in modern Psychoanalysis as Jungian analysis. This method and breakdown of Psychoanalysis is divided as Follows:


The Ego

This element of the psyche represents one’s own conscious mind. Everything that keeps track of one’s own personal narrative and continuity is contained, as well as how one identifies. Conscious emotion and memories are also a part of this, but this is not the whole story as one will find out.

The Persona

This is the mask that one puts on for the world. It is what allows us to be civilized in society and the reason that society can even exist. It is what sets expectation and lets us fill a role that can be defined explicitly like Doctor, Lawyer, Priest, ect. It hides our darker natures in order to protect one’s own image.

The Shadow

This element of the psyche represents all that one rejects within oneself. It is all the negative and unexamined aspects housed within the unconscious. The shadow can be projected outwards onto others in order to keep one’s ego protected. It is the mirror that shows us what we don’t like about ourselves so that perhaps one day we may face it.

The Anima/Animus

Jung posited that nested within the shadow there are manifestations of the opposite sex. These were termed Anima (feminine side of a man) and Animus (masculine side of a female). Each of these masculine and feminine energies are within oneself, and only by getting in touch with one’s opposite could one understand themselves fully.

Individuation

This is the process by which one integrates all that one has learned about oneself through careful introspection. It is only when the ego, persona, shadow, and Anima/Animus have been examined and integrated that one can finally reach their full potential as a human being.

The Self

Jung believed that the self was the ideal version of oneself. The version that is one and the same with the spark of divinity or god image. It is the reason that one seeks to improve instead of becoming bitter and corrupt. Jung believed that the point of life was to complete individuation so that the individual can stand against the masses while maintaining careful attention to also be apart of humanity as a whole.
